GLP-1 & Weight Loss

How Much Weight Can You Lose on Semaglutide? What to Expect

By Medcare Telehealth  •  2026-07-16  •  5 min read

If you’ve been exploring medical weight loss options, you’ve likely heard about semaglutide—the medication that’s changing how we think about managing weight. One of the first questions patients ask is: how much weight can you lose on semaglutide? It’s a fair question, and the answer depends on several individual factors. At Medcare Telehealth, our board-certified NPs in New York and Florida help patients understand what realistic, healthy weight loss looks like with this therapy.

What Is Semaglutide and How Does It Work?

Semaglutide is a GLP-1 receptor agonist, which means it mimics a natural hormone in your body that helps regulate appetite and blood sugar. By slowing stomach emptying and signaling fullness to your brain, semaglutide can help you eat less without feeling deprived. It was originally developed for type 2 diabetes, but higher doses are now FDA-approved for chronic weight management in adults with obesity or overweight-related health conditions.

Because it works with your body’s natural systems, results are gradual and sustainable—not a quick fix. That’s why understanding the typical weight loss range can help you set realistic expectations.

How Much Weight Can You Lose on Semaglutide: Average Results

Clinical studies show that people taking semaglutide for weight loss often lose 5% to 15% of their starting body weight over 6–12 months. For someone weighing 200 pounds, that’s roughly 10 to 30 pounds. Some individuals may lose more, especially when they combine the medication with healthy eating and physical activity. Others may lose less, depending on their metabolism, adherence, and overall health.

It’s important to remember that weight loss is not one-size-fits-all. Your results may vary, and that’s perfectly normal. The goal is consistent, healthy progress—not a number on the scale alone.

Timeline: What to Expect Month by Month

Weight loss on semaglutide typically happens in phases:

  • Weeks 1–4 (Starting dose): You may notice a small appetite shift. Weight loss is often modest—around 2–5 pounds—as your body adjusts.
  • Weeks 5–12 (Therapeutic dose): As the dose increases, appetite suppression becomes more noticeable. This is usually when more significant weight loss occurs, often 5–10 pounds per month for the first few months.
  • Months 4–6: Weight loss may slow slightly but often continues at a steady pace, averaging 1–2 pounds per week.
  • Months 6–12: Many patients continue losing weight or enter a maintenance phase. Total loss often reaches 10–15% of starting body weight.

Consistency with dosing, hydration, and nutrient-dense meals can help maintain momentum.

Factors That Influence Your Results

Several variables can affect how much weight you lose on semaglutide:

  • Starting weight and health conditions: People with higher starting BMIs often see more dramatic percentage losses.
  • Diet and activity level: Semaglutide is not a standalone solution. Pairing it with a balanced diet and regular movement amplifies results.
  • Consistency and dosing: Missing doses or not titrating as prescribed can slow progress.
  • Metabolic factors: Thyroid function, insulin resistance, and other hormones play a role.
  • Side effects: Some people experience nausea or fatigue early on, which can temporarily affect calorie intake or energy levels.

Your Medcare Telehealth NP will monitor your progress and adjust your plan as needed to optimize safety and effectiveness.

Realistic Expectations and Safety First

It’s tempting to focus on the highest possible number, but healthy weight loss is steady and sustainable. Rapid loss can lead to muscle wasting, gallstones, or nutritional deficiencies. Semaglutide is a tool—not a miracle—and it works best when you treat it as part of a comprehensive wellness plan.

Your provider will also watch for side effects and ensure the medication remains appropriate for you over time. Regular check-ins via telehealth make it easy to stay on track without disrupting your daily routine.
