How to Get a Lab Work Order from a Telehealth Doctor

By Medcare Telehealth  •  2026-07-04  •  5 min read

If you need blood work, urine tests, or other routine lab screening, you might wonder whether a virtual visit can help. The short answer is yes—it is often possible to get a lab work order from a telehealth doctor, especially when the test is part of routine monitoring, preventive care, or management of a chronic condition. At Medcare Telehealth, our board-certified nurse practitioners (licensed in NY and FL) can evaluate your symptoms and medical history remotely and, when appropriate, send lab orders to a lab near you.

What Is a Telehealth Lab Order?

A telehealth lab order is simply a lab requisition that your provider sends electronically to a laboratory after a virtual consultation. Instead of visiting a clinic in person, you join a secure video call with your practitioner. If a lab test is clinically appropriate, the provider may order blood draws, urine tests, or other panels. The lab work order from a telehealth doctor works just like one from an in-person visit—you take the order to a local lab facility, such as Labcorp or Quest Diagnostics, and a phlebotomist completes the draw.

When Can a Telehealth Provider Order Labs?

Not every lab test requires a physical exam. In many cases, a detailed history and discussion of your symptoms can help determine which tests are appropriate. Common examples where a virtual provider may order labs include:

  • Routine annual blood work (basic metabolic panel, lipid panel, complete blood count)
  • Thyroid function tests for monitoring hypothyroidism or hyperthyroidism
  • Hemoglobin A1c for diabetes management
  • Vitamin D or B12 levels to assess nutritional status
  • Urinalysis for possible urinary tract infections

Keep in mind that some tests may still require an in-person visit—for example, if a physical exam is essential for diagnosis. Your practitioner will always be honest about what can be managed remotely.

How the Process Works at Medcare Telehealth

Getting a lab work order from a telehealth doctor at Medcare Telehealth is straightforward. Here is what you can expect:

  • Schedule an appointment online through our secure booking system.
  • Meet with your NP via a private video visit. Your provider will review your symptoms, medical history, and any current medications.
  • If labs are warranted, the NP will send an electronic order to a lab location you choose. We often use national labs so you can find one close to home.
  • Go to the lab at your convenience. No appointment is usually needed for standard draws, though it is best to confirm with your local lab.
  • Receive results through your Medcare Telehealth patient portal. Your NP may then schedule a follow-up visit to discuss findings and next steps.

The entire process is designed to save you time while maintaining quality care. For many patients, a virtual consultation is far faster than waiting for an in-person appointment.

What to Prepare for Your Virtual Visit

To make sure your provider can determine whether a lab order is appropriate, it helps to be prepared. Before your video call, take a moment to gather the following:

  • A list of your current symptoms, including when they started and how they affect your daily life
  • Any recent lab results or medical records (if you have them)
  • A list of medications and supplements you are taking
  • Your pharmacy and insurance information

Being organized helps the NP make the best clinical decision for you. If you have questions about whether a specific test can be ordered remotely, you can always ask during the visit.

Is Telehealth Right for Your Lab Needs?

Telehealth has become a popular and effective way to manage many aspects of healthcare, including ordering lab tests. It is especially valuable if you have a busy schedule, limited mobility, or want to avoid exposure to illness in waiting rooms. A lab work order from a telehealth doctor can be a convenient bridge between staying home and getting the lab work you need. However, it is always important to work with a provider who takes a careful, ethical approach—ordering only the tests that are clinically relevant for your situation.

At Medcare Telehealth, our board-certified NPs are committed to giving you clear, patient-focused guidance. We do not over-promise or make guarantees. Instead, we evaluate each person individually and recommend what we believe is safest for your health. If lab work is part of that plan, we make it easy for you to follow through.
