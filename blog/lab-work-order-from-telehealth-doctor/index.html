Lab Work Order from Telehealth Doctor: What to Expect

By Medcare Telehealth  •  2026-06-11  •  5 min read

One of the most common questions patients ask is whether telehealth doctors can actually order lab work. The short answer is yes—and it's one of the most practical benefits of remote healthcare. As a board-certified nurse practitioner licensed in New York and Florida, I've ordered thousands of lab tests through telehealth visits, and I want to walk you through exactly how this process works.

Can Telehealth Doctors Really Order Lab Work?

Absolutely. Licensed telehealth providers, including physicians and nurse practitioners, have the same prescriptive authority whether they're seeing patients in person or remotely. That means a lab work order from a telehealth doctor is just as valid as one you'd receive at your local clinic.

During a telehealth visit, I can evaluate your symptoms, medical history, and current medications just as thoroughly as an in-person appointment. If lab work makes sense for your care—whether that's bloodwork, urinalysis, or other diagnostic tests—I can write the order and send it directly to a lab near you.

The key difference? You don't have to sit in a waiting room. You can have your appointment from home and then visit a nearby lab on your own schedule.

How the Lab Work Order Process Works

Here's what typically happens when you need lab work through Medcare Telehealth:

  • During your visit: We discuss your symptoms, health concerns, and any test you may need. I'll explain why specific lab work may be helpful for your situation.
  • Order placement: If lab tests are appropriate, I'll place the order electronically. You'll receive details about which tests were ordered and why.
  • Lab location: The order goes to a lab convenient for you. Most patients can visit within a few days, and many labs offer evening and weekend hours.
  • Sample collection: You visit the lab, provide your sample (usually blood or urine), and that's it. No appointment needed at most locations.
  • Results: Lab results typically come back within 24-72 hours, depending on the test. You can review them through your patient portal and we'll discuss findings at a follow-up visit if needed.

What Types of Lab Work Can Telehealth Doctors Order?

Telehealth doctors can order most routine lab work, including:

  • Complete blood counts and metabolic panels
  • Cholesterol and lipid panels
  • Thyroid function tests
  • Urinalysis and urine cultures
  • Blood glucose monitoring
  • Liver and kidney function tests
  • Vitamin and mineral level checks

More specialized testing may sometimes require an in-person visit or additional imaging, but for the majority of common diagnostic needs, telehealth ordering works seamlessly.

Why Telehealth Lab Orders Make Sense

Ordering lab work through telehealth can save you significant time. You avoid multiple trips—one visit to see your provider and another to the lab, often weeks apart. Everything moves faster because there's less scheduling friction.

For patients managing chronic conditions, this efficiency matters. Whether you're monitoring blood sugar, cholesterol, or thyroid levels, regular lab work is often the key to staying ahead of problems. Telehealth makes it easier to stay consistent with testing schedules.

There's also peace of mind knowing you can get lab work ordered without taking time off work or arranging transportation. For busy professionals, parents, or anyone with limited mobility, this is genuinely life-changing.

Getting Started With Lab Work Through Telehealth

If you think you may need lab work, the first step is scheduling a telehealth visit. Be ready to discuss your symptoms, health history, and any previous test results you have. This helps me determine what testing may be most useful for your situation.

The entire process—from booking your appointment to having results in hand—often takes less than a week. That's the practical advantage of modern telehealth combined with widespread lab access.
