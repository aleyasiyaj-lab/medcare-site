What Is Pink Eye and What Causes It?

Pink eye, also known as conjunctivitis, is an inflammation of the thin, clear tissue that lines the inside of your eyelid and covers the white part of your eye. It can make your eye appear pink or red and may be accompanied by itching, tearing, discharge, or a gritty feeling. Causes vary:

  • Viral conjunctivitis: Often associated with colds or upper respiratory infections. It can be very contagious.
  • Bacterial conjunctivitis: Caused by bacteria, it often produces a thicker, yellow-green discharge that may cause eyelids to stick together.
  • Allergic conjunctivitis: Triggered by allergens like pollen, dust, or pet dander. It usually affects both eyes and may be seasonal.

While pink eye can be uncomfortable, most cases are not serious. However, proper diagnosis is important because treatment depends on the cause.

What to Expect During Your Virtual Pink Eye Visit

When you book an appointment at Medcare Telehealth, you’ll connect with a board-certified nurse practitioner who is licensed in NY and FL. Here’s how the process typically works:

  • Share your symptoms: You’ll be asked about redness, discharge, itching, pain, and whether you have other symptoms like a cold or fever.
  • Provide visual details: Your provider may ask you to take a clear photo of your eye using your phone’s camera. This can be very helpful for assessment.
  • Discuss your medical history: Let your provider know about any allergies, recent illnesses, or contact lens use.
  • Receive guidance: Based on your symptoms, your provider will suggest next steps. For bacterial cases, they may prescribe antibiotic eye drops. For viral or allergic causes, they may recommend artificial tears, antihistamine drops, cold compresses, or other supportive care.

If your symptoms are severe, if you have vision changes, or if you have a weakened immune system, your provider may recommend an in-person evaluation. But for most uncomplicated cases, a virtual visit is both safe and effective.

When to See a Doctor for Pink Eye

While many cases of pink eye improve on their own, certain symptoms warrant prompt medical attention. We encourage you to seek care if you experience:

  • Moderate to severe eye pain
  • Blurred or decreased vision
  • Sensitivity to light (photophobia)
  • Intense redness in one or both eyes
  • Symptoms that worsen or do not improve after 24–48 hours
