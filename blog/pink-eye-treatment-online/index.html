What Is Pink Eye and Why Does It Happen?

Conjunctivitis is inflammation of the conjunctiva—the clear membrane covering the white part of your eye and inner eyelids. This inflammation can cause redness, itching, discharge, and discomfort that can make it hard to focus on daily tasks.

Pink eye can develop from several causes:

  • Viral infections - often spread through respiratory droplets or direct contact, commonly associated with colds
  • Bacterial infections - may produce thicker discharge and can sometimes require antibiotic treatment
  • Allergic reactions - triggered by pollen, pet dander, or environmental irritants
  • Chemical irritation - from chlorine, smoke, or other substances

Determining the underlying cause is essential because the right treatment depends on what's causing your symptoms.

Why Choose Online Pink Eye Treatment?

Telehealth appointments for pink eye offer real advantages over waiting in a crowded urgent care clinic. First, you reduce your exposure to other illnesses during your visit. Second, you can be evaluated quickly—often within hours of booking. Third, if a prescription is needed, it can typically be sent directly to your pharmacy for convenient pickup.

During your online visit, our licensed providers can review your symptoms, look at images you share, and discuss your medical history to guide an appropriate treatment plan. While we cannot physically examine your eye under magnification through a screen, many cases of pink eye can be effectively managed with a thorough telehealth consultation.

Managing Pink Eye at Home

While waiting for your appointment or following your provider's recommendations, certain self-care measures may help ease discomfort:

  • Use warm or cool compresses on the affected eye several times daily
  • Practice good hygiene—wash your hands frequently and avoid touching your eyes
  • Remove contact lenses and stick to glasses until symptoms resolve
  • Use clean towels and washcloths, never sharing with others
  • Avoid eye makeup until the infection clears

These steps can reduce irritation and help prevent spreading the infection to your other eye or to people around you.

When to Seek Urgent In-Person Care

While telehealth works well for many pink eye cases, certain situations warrant immediate in-person evaluation. Contact an emergency room or urgent care clinic if you experience vision changes, severe eye pain, sensitivity to light beyond normal pink eye discomfort, or if symptoms don't improve after a few days of treatment.
