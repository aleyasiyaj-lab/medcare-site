GLP-1 & Weight Loss

GLP-1 Side Effects: What to Expect

By Medcare Telehealth  •  2026-05-29  •  5 min read

If you're considering GLP-1 medications for weight loss or blood sugar management, understanding potential side effects is an important part of making an informed decision. As a board-certified nurse practitioner, I work with patients daily who are starting GLP-1 therapy, and one of the most common questions I hear is: "What should I actually expect?" Let's walk through what you need to know.

Common GLP-1 Side Effects

Most patients experience mild to moderate side effects when beginning GLP-1 medications like semaglutide or tirzepatide. The good news is that many of these effects tend to improve over time as your body adjusts to the medication.

The most frequently reported side effects include:

  • Nausea – Often the most noticeable effect, especially in the first few weeks. This may be worse in the morning or after eating.
  • Constipation or diarrhea – These gastrointestinal changes are common as GLP-1s slow digestion.
  • Reduced appetite – This is actually the intended effect, though some patients find it more pronounced than expected.
  • Fatigue – Some patients report feeling tired, particularly when first starting treatment.
  • Headaches – These can occur but typically subside within a few weeks.

Most side effects are mild and decrease significantly after the first month of treatment. However, severity varies from person to person, which is why personalized medical guidance matters.

Managing Side Effects Effectively

There are practical strategies that can help minimize discomfort while your body adapts. Start with eating smaller, more frequent meals—this can ease nausea considerably. Many patients find that avoiding high-fat or greasy foods also helps during the adjustment period.

Staying well-hydrated is essential, especially if you're experiencing constipation or diarrhea. I typically recommend my patients drink at least 8-10 glasses of water daily. You might also consider increasing fiber intake gradually, though this should be balanced with hydration.

Ginger tea, peppermint, or anti-nausea medications may provide relief during the first few weeks. Simple lifestyle adjustments like eating slowly, taking your time between bites, and avoiding strong-smelling foods can also make a meaningful difference in how you feel.

When to Contact Your Provider

While most side effects are temporary and manageable, some symptoms warrant immediate medical attention. Contact your healthcare provider if you experience severe abdominal pain, persistent vomiting, signs of dehydration, or if side effects don't improve after two weeks of treatment.

Additionally, certain individuals may have contraindications or need dose adjustments. This is why working with a qualified healthcare provider who understands your complete medical history is crucial. At Medcare Telehealth, we monitor patients closely during their first month of GLP-1 treatment to ensure you're tolerating the medication well and making adjustments as needed.

The Timeline for Improvement

Most patients notice that side effects peak in the first 1-2 weeks, then gradually improve. By week 3-4, many experience significantly fewer symptoms. Some patients report feeling completely normal by their second month of treatment.

That said, your individual timeline may differ. Age, metabolism, overall health, and the specific medication prescribed all play a role in how quickly you adapt. Patience and consistent communication with your provider help ensure your experience is as smooth as possible.

The side effects of GLP-1 medications are generally manageable and temporary, and the benefits many patients experience can be significant.
