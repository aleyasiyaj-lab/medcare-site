How Does Telehealth Work? A Simple Guide for Patients

By Medcare Telehealth  •  2026-07-05  •  5 min read

If you’ve ever wondered how does telehealth work, you’re not alone. More patients are turning to virtual care for its convenience, privacy, and quick access to a healthcare provider. Whether you’re managing a chronic condition or dealing with a minor illness, telehealth can often connect you to a board-certified nurse practitioner from the comfort of your home.

At Medcare Telehealth, we’re licensed in New York and Florida, and our mission is to make healthcare simple, safe, and accessible. Let’s walk through exactly what to expect when you choose a telehealth visit.

What Is Telehealth and How Does It Work?

How does telehealth work? At its core, telehealth is a live, secure video appointment between you and a healthcare provider—using your smartphone, tablet, or computer. It’s not a recorded call or a chat bot; it’s a real-time visit where your provider can see you, listen to your concerns, and offer guidance.

You don’t need any special software. Just a stable internet connection, a quiet spot, and your device’s camera and microphone. Many patients find it surprisingly similar to an in-office visit, but without the commute or waiting room.

Step-by-Step: Your First Telehealth Visit

Here’s exactly what a typical appointment looks like at Medcare Telehealth:

  • Step 1: Choose an appointment. Visit medcaretelehealth.org and select a time that fits your schedule. We offer same-day and next-day slots in many cases.
  • Step 2: Complete your check-in. You’ll fill out a brief health history and consent form online—no clipboard needed. This helps your NP understand your background before you connect.
  • Step 3: Join the video call. At your appointment time, click the secure link we email or text you. The connection is encrypted to protect your privacy.
  • Step 4: Talk with your NP. Your board-certified nurse practitioner will listen, ask questions, and discuss next steps. This may include lifestyle advice, a medication recommendation, or a referral for labs or in-person care if needed.
  • Step 5: Follow-up plan. After your visit, we send a summary and any prescriptions directly to your pharmacy—all without you leaving the house.

What Can Telehealth Be Used For?

How does telehealth work for different conditions? Telehealth can be a good option for many common concerns, including:

  • Cold and flu symptoms
  • Allergies or sinus issues
  • Urinary tract infections (UTIs)
  • Skin rashes or mild infections (with photos)
  • Anxiety, depression, or stress management
  • Medication refills for ongoing conditions
  • Follow-up visits after a recent illness or procedure

It’s important to note that not all situations are suitable for virtual care. If you’re experiencing chest pain, severe bleeding, or a life-threatening emergency, please call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room. Your NP will always advise if an in-person visit is more appropriate.

Is Telehealth Safe and Private?

Yes. Telehealth platforms must comply with strict federal privacy laws (like HIPAA) to protect your health information. At Medcare Telehealth, your video session is never recorded or shared without your permission. We use encrypted connections so your conversation stays between you and your NP.

Additionally, your provider is a board-certified nurse practitioner with years of clinical experience—licensed specifically in New York and Florida. You’re getting the same expertise you’d expect in a clinic, delivered virtually.

Why Patients Choose Telehealth Over In-Person Visits

Many people find virtual care to be a more comfortable and efficient option. Here are a few common reasons:

  • No travel time. Skip traffic, parking, and waiting rooms.
  • Flexible scheduling. Appointments are available during hours that work for you—including evenings and weekends.
  • Less exposure to germs. Avoid crowded waiting areas, especially during cold and flu season.
  • Lower cost. Telehealth visits often cost less than in-person appointments, and many insurance plans cover them.
