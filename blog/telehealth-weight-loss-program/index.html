GLP-1 & Weight Loss

Telehealth Weight Loss Program: GLP-1 Therapy Explained

By Medcare Telehealth  •  2026-06-17  •  5 min read

If you've been exploring weight loss options, you've likely heard about GLP-1 medications and telehealth weight loss programs. The combination of convenient virtual care and evidence-based treatment can be transformative, but it's important to understand how these programs work and what to expect. As a board-certified nurse practitioner, I want to walk you through what a telehealth weight loss program actually involves and how it may support your health goals.

What Is a Telehealth Weight Loss Program?

A telehealth weight loss program brings personalized weight management care directly to your home through secure video visits. Rather than traveling to an office, you meet with licensed healthcare providers who evaluate your medical history, current health status, and weight loss goals. These programs often combine medication management with nutritional guidance and behavioral support—all delivered virtually.

This approach can be particularly effective because it removes barriers to care. You get professional oversight without scheduling conflicts or commute time, making consistency easier to maintain.

Understanding GLP-1 Therapy

GLP-1 receptor agonists are medications originally developed for type 2 diabetes management. These medications work by mimicking a natural hormone your body produces to help regulate blood sugar and appetite. When prescribed for weight management, GLP-1 medications may help by:

  • Reducing hunger signals in the brain
  • Increasing feelings of fullness and satisfaction
  • Slowing stomach emptying to extend satiety
  • Supporting more stable blood sugar levels

It's crucial to understand that these medications are not a substitute for lifestyle changes. Instead, they can make it significantly easier to stick with healthier eating patterns and exercise routines by addressing the biological factors that drive appetite and cravings.

What to Expect From Your Program

When you start a telehealth weight loss program at Medcare, the process typically begins with a comprehensive evaluation. We review your complete medical history, current medications, previous weight loss attempts, and any underlying health conditions. This information helps us determine if GLP-1 therapy is appropriate for you and which medication might work best.

If you're a good candidate, we'll start at a low dose and gradually increase it over several weeks. During this time, you'll have regular virtual check-ins to monitor your progress, discuss any side effects you're experiencing, and adjust your treatment plan as needed. Most patients can expect some common side effects early on—like mild nausea or changes in appetite—but these often improve as your body adjusts.

Beyond medication, our program includes guidance on nutrition and realistic lifestyle adjustments. We don't believe in restrictive diets or unsustainable exercise plans. Instead, we focus on gradual, sustainable changes that fit into your actual life.

Why Choose a Telehealth Approach?

Telehealth weight loss programs offer several advantages over in-person-only care. You have flexibility in scheduling, especially important if you work full-time or have family responsibilities. You also benefit from the accountability and expertise of licensed providers without geographic limitations. Whether you're in New York or Florida, you can access board-certified care from experienced professionals.

Additionally, telehealth programs create a low-pressure environment where many patients feel more comfortable discussing their weight management journey honestly. You're in a comfortable space—often your own home—which can reduce anxiety about these conversations.

Is a Telehealth Weight Loss Program Right for You?

The best way to know is to have a conversation with a qualified provider. Not everyone is a candidate for GLP-1 therapy, and that's okay. We have other evidence-based approaches available. What matters most is finding the right fit for your unique situation, medical history, and goals.
