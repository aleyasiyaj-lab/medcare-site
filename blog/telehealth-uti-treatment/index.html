What Is a UTI and Why Does It Need Prompt Care?

A urinary tract infection happens when bacteria enter the urinary system—most commonly the bladder. Symptoms can include frequent urination, pain or burning during urination, cloudy or strong-smelling urine, and pelvic pressure. While mild UTIs may resolve on their own, many require attention to prevent the infection from spreading to the kidneys. Prompt treatment can help you feel better faster and may reduce the risk of complications.

How a Telehealth UTI Visit Works at Medcare Telehealth

Our process is designed to be straightforward and patient-focused. Here’s what you can expect:

  • Step 1: Book online. Visit medcaretelehealth.org/#book and choose a time that works for you.
  • Step 2: Connect with your provider. During the video visit, your NP will ask about your symptoms, medical history, and any medications you’re taking.

What to Prepare for Your Visit

To make the most of your telehealth UTI treatment appointment, have the following ready:

  • A list of your symptoms and when they started
  • Any recent UTI history or related conditions (like diabetes or pregnancy)
  • Current medications, including supplements
  • A quiet, private space with a reliable internet connection

Your NP will guide you through the rest. Open communication helps us provide the most accurate care possible.

Frequently Asked Questions About Telehealth for UTIs

Is it safe to treat a UTI via telehealth? Yes, for many uncomplicated cases. Telehealth allows your provider to evaluate symptoms and history thoroughly. If your case is more complex, your NP will advise on next steps, which may include lab work or an in-person visit.

Will I need to provide a urine sample? Not always. For straightforward symptoms, a detailed history may be enough. Your provider will let you know if a test is necessary.

Can I get antibiotics prescribed online? If appropriate, your NP can send a prescription to your local pharmacy. However, treatment plans vary based on your individual health needs.
