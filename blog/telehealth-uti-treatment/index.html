Urinary tract infections (UTIs) are one of the most common reasons people seek urgent care. The burning, the urgency, the discomfort — it can disrupt your entire day. But you don’t have to sit in a crowded waiting room to get relief. With Medcare Telehealth, you can receive expert telehealth UTI treatment from a board-certified nurse practitioner in New York or Florida, all from the comfort of your home.

What Is Telehealth for UTIs?

Telehealth allows you to meet with a licensed healthcare provider through a secure video visit on your phone, tablet, or computer. For uncomplicated UTIs — those without fever, back pain, or other complicated symptoms — a virtual visit may be a safe and effective first step. During your appointment, our NP will review your symptoms, medical history, and any recent lab results. If appropriate, they may prescribe antibiotics to a pharmacy near you. This approach can save you time and reduce unnecessary exposure to illness.

Common UTI Symptoms We Address

While every person is different, UTIs often cause:

  • A persistent urge to urinate even when your bladder is empty
  • A burning sensation during urination
  • Cloudy, dark, or strong-smelling urine
  • Pelvic pressure or lower abdominal discomfort

It’s important to note that not all urinary symptoms are caused by a UTI. Conditions like vaginal infections, interstitial cystitis, or sexually transmitted infections can mimic UTI symptoms. That’s why a thorough evaluation by a knowledgeable provider is key. Your Medcare NP will help determine the most likely cause and recommend next steps.

When Is Telehealth UTI Treatment Appropriate?

Telehealth is best suited for uncomplicated UTIs in otherwise healthy adults. You may be a candidate for a virtual visit if:

  • You are not pregnant
  • You do not have a fever, chills, or nausea
  • You do not have significant back or flank pain
  • You are male (male UTIs often require a different approach, so your NP may recommend an in-person evaluation)

If you have recurrent UTIs, diabetes, or a history of kidney issues, your NP may suggest additional testing or an in-person visit to ensure the safest care. The goal is not to treat everyone virtually, but to provide the right care for your unique situation.

What to Expect During Your Virtual Visit

Getting started is simple. First, you’ll book an appointment at medcaretelehealth.org/#book. Before the visit, you will fill out a brief health questionnaire so your NP can prepare. During the video call, you’ll discuss your symptoms and any relevant medical history. The appointment typically lasts 10–15 minutes. If a prescription is indicated, it will be sent electronically to your preferred pharmacy. Most patients receive their medication within an hour or two.

When to Seek In-Person Care

While telehealth can be a great starting point, certain signs warrant immediate medical attention. If you experience high fever, shaking chills, nausea or vomiting, severe back or side pain, or blood in your urine, please visit an emergency room or urgent care center right away. These symptoms may indicate a kidney infection, which requires in-person evaluation and often intravenous antibiotics.

At Medcare Telehealth, your safety comes first. If your NP determines that an in-person visit is necessary, we will guide you on the next steps.
