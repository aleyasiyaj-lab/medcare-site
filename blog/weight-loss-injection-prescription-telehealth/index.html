What Are Weight Loss Injections?

Weight loss injections, often belonging to a class of drugs called GLP-1 receptor agonists, work by mimicking a hormone that helps regulate appetite and blood sugar. Medications like semaglutide and liraglutide are commonly prescribed under medical supervision. These treatments are not a quick fix; they are tools that may support long-term weight management when combined with lifestyle changes. A board-certified healthcare provider needs to evaluate your health history and goals before issuing a prescription.

Who May Be a Candidate for a Weight Loss Injection?

These prescription medications are typically intended for individuals with a body mass index (BMI) of 30 or higher, or those with a BMI of 27 or higher who have at least one weight-related condition, such as high blood pressure, type 2 diabetes, or high cholesterol. However, each person is unique. During your virtual visit, your Medcare Telehealth provider will discuss your specific situation, including any potential risks or side effects, to help you make an informed decision.

It's important to note that these injections are not for everyone. Pregnant or breastfeeding women, people with certain thyroid conditions, or those with a history of pancreatitis may not be suitable candidates. A thorough consultation helps ensure safety.

What to Expect After You Start Treatment

If you and your provider decide a weight loss injection prescription is appropriate, you will receive clear instructions on how to use the medication. Most injections are given once weekly, and dosing usually starts low and gradually increases. Common side effects may include nausea, digestive discomfort, or fatigue, but these often improve over time.
