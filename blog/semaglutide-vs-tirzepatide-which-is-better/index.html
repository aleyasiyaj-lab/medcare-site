GLP-1 & Weight Loss

Semaglutide vs Tirzepatide: Which Is Better for Weight Loss?

By Medcare Telehealth  •  2026-07-12  •  5 min read

If you’ve been exploring medical weight loss options, you’ve likely come across two popular medications: semaglutide and tirzepatide. Both are GLP-1 receptor agonists that can help with weight management, but they work slightly differently. In this guide, we’ll compare semaglutide vs tirzepatide to help you understand which may be a better fit for your health goals. Please note: this information is for educational purposes and should not replace a conversation with your healthcare provider.

What Are GLP-1 Receptor Agonists?

GLP-1 receptor agonists are a class of medications originally developed to manage type 2 diabetes. They mimic a natural hormone called glucagon-like peptide-1, which helps regulate blood sugar and appetite. By slowing gastric emptying and increasing feelings of fullness, these medications can support significant weight loss when combined with lifestyle changes.

Both semaglutide (brand names: Ozempic, Wegovy) and tirzepatide (brand name: Mounjaro, Zepbound) fall into this category, but tirzepatide has an additional mechanism of action.

Key Differences: Semaglutide vs Tirzepatide

While both drugs target GLP-1 receptors, tirzepatide also activates GIP (glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide) receptors. This dual action may lead to enhanced blood sugar control and, in some studies, greater weight loss outcomes. Here are the primary differences:

  • Mechanism of action: Semaglutide is a GLP-1 receptor agonist only. Tirzepatide is a dual GLP-1/GIP receptor agonist.
  • Weight loss potential: In clinical trials, tirzepatide has shown average weight loss of up to 22% of body weight, while semaglutide has shown up to 15%. Individual results vary widely.
  • Dosing: Both are injectable medications taken once weekly, but dosing schedules and maximum doses differ slightly.
  • Side effects: Both may cause nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, or constipation, especially when starting. Tirzepatide may have a slightly higher rate of gastrointestinal side effects in some patients.

It's important to note that neither medication is “better” for everyone. Your personal health history, tolerance, and goals ultimately determine which option your provider may recommend.

How Do I Choose Between Semaglutide and Tirzepatide?

Choosing between semaglutide vs tirzepatide depends on several factors:

  • Your weight loss goals: If you have more weight to lose, tirzepatide may offer greater average reductions, but semaglutide is also highly effective.
  • Insurance coverage: Some insurance plans may cover one medication but not the other. We can help you check your benefits.
  • Side effect tolerance: If you are sensitive to gastrointestinal symptoms, your provider might start with semaglutide and a slow titration schedule.
  • Cost: Without insurance, tirzepatide can be more expensive. Generic options are not yet available for either.

Ultimately, the decision should be made in partnership with a board-certified practitioner who can evaluate your medical history and preferences.

What to Expect When You Start Treatment

Whether you choose semaglutide or tirzepatide, both require a gradual dose increase over several weeks to minimize side effects. You can expect regular check-ins with your provider, as well as guidance on nutrition and physical activity. Weight loss with GLP-1 medications is typically slow and steady, and most patients see noticeable results within the first few months.

Remember: these medications are tools, not magic. Long-term success depends on sustainable habits like balanced eating, regular movement, and adequate sleep.
