GLP-1 & Weight Loss

Semaglutide vs Tirzepatide: Which Is Better for Weight Loss?

By Medcare Telehealth  •  2026-07-07  •  5 min read

If you’ve been researching weight loss medications, you’ve likely come across two names: semaglutide and tirzepatide. Both are GLP-1 receptor agonists that have shown impressive results in clinical studies. But deciding between them can feel overwhelming. As a board-certified NP with Medcare Telehealth (licensed in NY and FL), I’m here to break down what you need to know in a clear, patient-focused way.

How Do Semaglutide and Tirzepatide Work?

Both medications mimic natural hormones that regulate appetite and blood sugar. Semaglutide targets one receptor (GLP-1), which slows digestion and signals fullness to the brain. Tirzepatide targets two receptors—GLP-1 and GIP—potentially offering a dual-action approach. This difference may influence how well each works for weight loss and blood sugar control.

Effectiveness for Weight Loss

Clinical trials show that both medications can lead to significant weight loss, but tirzepatide has demonstrated slightly higher average reductions. In head-to-head studies, tirzepatide users lost about 15–20% of their body weight on average, compared to 10–15% with semaglutide. However, individual results vary. Your response may depend on factors like diet, activity level, and metabolic health. Neither medication is a magic bullet—they work best as part of a comprehensive plan.

Side Effects and Tolerability

Both semaglutide and tirzepatide share similar side effects, including nausea, diarrhea, vomiting, and constipation—especially when starting or increasing the dose. These often improve over time. Some patients find that the dual action of tirzepatide causes slightly more gastrointestinal discomfort initially. It’s important to start at a low dose and increase gradually under medical supervision. We always monitor patients closely to help manage any side effects.

Cost and Insurance Coverage

Cost is a key factor. Brand-name semaglutide (Ozempic, Wegovy) and tirzepatide (Mounjaro, Zepbound) can be expensive, often $800–$1,300 per month without insurance. Insurance coverage varies widely. Semaglutide has been on the market longer, so some plans may cover it more readily. Our team at Medcare Telehealth can help you explore coverage options and any available savings programs. Compounded formulations may be more affordable, but we always discuss risks and benefits with you.

Which One Is Right for You?

The answer depends on your health goals, medical history, and personal preferences. If you have diabetes, tirzepatide may offer better A1C control. If you’re focused solely on weight loss and want a medication with extensive safety data, semaglutide is a proven choice. We consider factors like kidney function, thyroid history, and past reactions to medications. A one-size-fits-all approach doesn’t work here—that’s why a personalized consultation is so important.
