What Is a Sinus Infection and When Should You Seek Care?

A sinus infection, or sinusitis, happens when the tissues lining your sinuses become inflamed. This can be triggered by a cold, allergies, or bacterial infection. While some sinus infections resolve on their own, others benefit from prompt medical evaluation.

You may want to consider online care if you're experiencing:

  • Facial pain or pressure around the cheeks and forehead
  • Thick nasal discharge (yellow, green, or clear)
  • Nasal congestion lasting more than 10 days
  • Headache and fatigue
  • Cough, especially at night

Getting evaluated quickly can help determine whether you need treatment and prevent symptoms from worsening.

How Online Sinus Infection Treatment Works

At Medcare Telehealth, our board-certified nurse practitioners can evaluate your symptoms via secure video consultation—often the same day you request an appointment. During your visit, your provider will:

  • Ask detailed questions about your symptoms and how long you've had them
  • Discuss any recent colds, allergies, or other relevant medical history
  • Review your symptoms to determine if treatment is needed
  • Discuss management options tailored to your situation

If appropriate, your provider can send a prescription directly to your pharmacy, and you'll have guidance on over-the-counter options and self-care strategies. No need to take time off work, sit in a waiting room, or reschedule your day.

Treatment Options Your Provider May Discuss

While we can't guarantee specific outcomes, treatment for sinus infections may include:

  • Medications: Depending on whether your infection appears viral or bacterial, your provider may recommend antibiotics, decongestants, nasal steroids, or antihistamines
  • Home care: Saline rinses, humidifiers, and warm compresses often help reduce congestion and facial pressure
  • Symptom management: Over-the-counter pain relievers and cough suppressants can make you more comfortable while your body heals

Your provider will explain which options may work best for your specific situation and answer any questions you have about your care plan.
