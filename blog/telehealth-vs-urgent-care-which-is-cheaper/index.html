Telehealth vs Urgent Care: Which is Cheaper?

By Medcare Telehealth  •  2026-06-04  •  5 min read

When you're not feeling well, cost is often a major concern alongside getting the care you need. You've likely heard about both telehealth and urgent care, but understanding how their prices compare can help you make a smarter decision for your health and finances. Let's break down what you should know about telehealth versus urgent care costs.

Understanding Telehealth Pricing

Telehealth visits typically range from $50 to $150 per appointment, depending on your provider and location. Many telehealth companies, including Medcare Telehealth, offer transparent pricing upfront, so you know exactly what to expect before you book. These visits are conducted through video, phone, or secure messaging with licensed healthcare providers—no waiting rooms, no travel time.

What makes telehealth particularly cost-effective is what you're not paying for. There's no facility overhead passed to you, no expensive medical equipment being used, and no lengthy appointment slots that increase costs. You're paying primarily for the provider's expertise and time. Additionally, many insurance plans cover telehealth visits at the same rate as in-person office visits, and some offer lower copays for virtual care.

What Urgent Care Actually Costs

Urgent care visits generally range from $150 to $300 without insurance, often significantly higher than telehealth. This higher cost reflects the facility expenses: staffing, equipment, waiting areas, and overhead. With insurance, you'll typically pay a copay (usually $75-$150), but the total bill submitted to your insurer can be substantially more.

Beyond the visit itself, urgent care centers may charge additional fees for services like X-rays, lab tests, or wound care. These extras can quickly inflate your final bill. Travel time and waiting—sometimes 30 minutes to two hours—are also costs to consider, particularly if you're taking time off work.

When Telehealth Is the More Affordable Choice

Telehealth tends to be significantly cheaper for non-emergency conditions like:

  • Cold and flu symptoms
  • Sore throat and mild infections
  • Skin rashes or minor irritations
  • Medication refills and management
  • Mental health consultations
  • Follow-up appointments after in-person care

For these issues, a telehealth provider can often diagnose and treat you without needing physical examination or advanced testing. A board-certified nurse practitioner or physician can assess symptoms, answer questions, and may prescribe medications when appropriate—all from your home at a fraction of the cost.

The Real Cost Comparison

Here's a practical example: A telehealth visit for a suspected urinary tract infection might cost you $75-$100 out of pocket, plus a potential $20-$30 prescription fee. An urgent care visit for the same issue could easily cost $200-$300 before any lab work or additional services. Over time, choosing telehealth for appropriate conditions can result in significant savings.

That said, urgent care remains necessary and justified for certain situations—like suspected fractures, severe injuries, or conditions requiring physical examination or imaging. The key is matching the right care setting to your actual health needs.

Hidden Savings with Telehealth

Beyond the direct visit cost, telehealth offers financial advantages that often go overlooked. You save on transportation, parking, and childcare needs. You don't lose work hours or productivity sitting in a waiting room. For working parents or people without reliable transportation, these indirect savings can be substantial.

Many employers also offer telehealth benefits as part of their health plans, sometimes covering visits entirely or at a reduced rate. It's worth checking your benefits to see what telehealth options your insurance includes.

When you need quick, affordable medical care for common non-emergency concerns, telehealth represents a smarter financial choice for most people.
