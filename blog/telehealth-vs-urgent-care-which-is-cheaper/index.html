Telehealth vs Urgent Care: Which Is Cheaper?

By Medcare Telehealth  •  2026-07-11  •  5 min read

When you wake up with a sore throat, a nagging cough, or a mild rash, your first thought might be, "Do I go to urgent care or try a telehealth visit?" And if you are like most people, the next question is, "Which is cheaper?"

At Medcare Telehealth, we hear this every day. As a board-certified nurse practitioner licensed in New York and Florida, I want to help you understand the real cost differences between telehealth and urgent care so you can make an informed choice for your health and your wallet.

Understanding the Cost of Urgent Care

Urgent care centers are convenient for non-emergency issues like sprains, minor infections, and colds. But convenience often comes with a price. Here is a breakdown of typical urgent care costs:

  • Visit fee: $100 to $200, depending on your location and the complexity of your issue.
  • Insurance co-pay: For insured patients, co-pays typically range from $25 to $75, though some plans may require a coinsurance payment.
  • Hidden costs: Lab work, X-rays, or additional testing can add $50 to $300 or more to your bill.
  • Time cost: Average wait times at urgent care clinics are 30 to 60 minutes, not including travel time. Lost wages or childcare can also add up.

Understanding the Cost of Telehealth

Telehealth visits through services like Medcare Telehealth are designed to be straightforward and affordable. Here is what you can typically expect:

  • Visit fee: Many telehealth platforms offer visits ranging from $45 to $99, often lower than urgent care cash prices.
  • Insurance co-pay: Many insurance plans cover telehealth at the same rate as a primary care visit, or even with a lower co-pay. Some employers offer zero-co-pay telehealth options.
  • No hidden fees: Virtual visits do not include facility fees or added charges for simple evaluations. If labs or prescriptions are needed, those costs are separate and often comparable to outpatient rates.
  • Time saved: Average telehealth visits take 10 to 20 minutes. No driving, no waiting room, no parking fees. That time saved is real money for many families.

Telehealth vs Urgent Care: Which Is Cheaper Overall?

When comparing telehealth vs urgent care which is cheaper, the answer often depends on your specific situation. However, for many common conditions—such as sinus infections, urinary tract infections, mild allergies, or skin rashes—telehealth is typically the more affordable option.

Let me share a typical comparison: If you have insurance but need a quick evaluation for a sinus infection, an urgent care visit might cost a $50 co-pay plus a possible $20 lab fee. A telehealth visit for the same issue might be a $25 co-pay with no extra fees. For uninsured patients, the difference is even starker: an urgent care visit could be $150, while a telehealth visit might be $59.

However, urgent care may be the better choice for conditions that require a physical exam, such as a suspected fracture, a deep cut needing stitches, or a severe ear infection that needs a look inside the ear. In those cases, the cost of a virtual visit may be wasted if you ultimately need to be seen in person.

Also consider the cost of convenience: A telehealth visit can happen during your lunch break or while your kids are napping. That flexibility can save you from missing work or paying for last-minute childcare.

When to Choose Telehealth Over Urgent Care

Telehealth is an excellent choice for:

  • Mild illnesses: Cold, flu symptoms, allergies, cough, sore throat
  • Minor infections: Urinary tract infections, pink eye, sinus infections
  • Skin issues: Rashes, bug bites, mild acne, poison ivy
  • Chronic condition management: Medication refills, follow-ups for high blood pressure or diabetes
  • General questions: Need a doctor's note, travel advice, or a second opinion? A telehealth visit is quick and affordable.
